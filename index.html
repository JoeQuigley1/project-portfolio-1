What is BJJ?

The word “jiu jitsu” derives from the Japanese “Jū” meaning “gentle” and “Jutsu” meaning “art”; essentially, jiu jitsu is the “gentle art”. Brazilian Jiu Jitsu is a predominantly ground-based martial art, using the principals of leverage, angles, pressure and timing, as well as knowledge of the human anatomy, in order to achieve a non-violent submission of one’s opponent.


History

The Brazilian style of jiu jitsu resulted from the import of Japanese jiu jitsu and judo to the South American continent in the early 1900s, and its transformation into a new martial art style by a core group of Brazilian pioneers. Before it arrived to the shores of Brazil, however, the origins of jiu jitsu are believed to go back thousands of years.


Why do BJJ?

A short list of reasons how BJJ is great:

Healthy Body and Mind

1) Body

Martial Arts are a fantastic way to become more active and lose some weight. On average a 1 hour session of BJJ can burn in excess of 500 calories

As well as being a great workout BJJ improves flexibility and conditioning through constant technique drilling and sparrring sessions.

2) Mind

Brazilian Jiu Jitsu is sometimes referred to as the "game of human chess"

Training BJJ involves a huge amount of menatl concentrationwhile drilling techniques andputting them to use on the mat.

How and When to use it

MMA

Brazilian Jiu Jitsu is one of the most effective martial arts. This can be seen by its prevelance in mma.

In a fight that goes to ground, knowing BJJ is a huge advatage as it will give you the skills and technique to be in control of an opponent.

Self Defense

BJJ focuses on technique and the manipulation of your opponents weight. This is one of the reasons why it is especially popular among women as a form of self defense

On top of this, one of the main focuses of BJJ is rolling or sparring which gives the practitioner "real life" expeperience of incapacitating an opponent.

Confidence

5) Anyone can start

Brazilian Jiu Jitsu is a sport that anyone can do. Whether you are in your 20s or even your 60s a man, woman or a child, you can start to practive BJJ

Because it focuses on technique, even much smaller or less powerful practitioners can subdue opponents who are much larger.

What you learn on the mat doesn't just stay on the mat

With BJJ you are constatly challenging youself to improve and giving 100% on the mat. This ethos helps practinioners to go out into the world and be successful

Self-improvement

Promotes continuous improvement

Whether you are a black belt or a white belt, Brazilian Jiu Jitsu shows us that you always have more to learn. There are so many technique to learn and different ways to adapt them depending on your opponent that practinioners of BJJ are constantly learning and honing their skills.

Self improvement becomes a habit that many BJJ practinioners come away with.

Builds Character

Doing BJJ will make you fitter and stronger physically but it will also make you mentally strong. It is hard to step onto the mat after a long day at work or school so anyone who trains regularly will become resilient.

Constant ups and downs of BJJ means that you take failure as a lesson and move on from it.
